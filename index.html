International Widows Day

"For many women, becoming a widow does not just mean the heartache of losing a husband, but often losing everything else as well." -- Cherie Blair

Approxiximately 700,000 women become a widow in the U.S. each year.

They tend to live 14 years longer: more than a decade.

This may be 13.6 million in America

Divorce is the second most stressful event in life; death of a spouse, NUMBER ONE.

Widows are 66% more likely to die in the next three months, 'widowhood effect.'

Poverty: 3-4x greater than married peers.

The median income for women over 65 in America is $17,000

1/3 of widows are widowed before 60.

1/3 of those 65 and older are widowed

Harder for widows to remarry because the pool of single men is smaller than single women.

Older men with money also prefer younger women, so old widows have a very hard time attracting a spouse.

To marry a poor, single man her age would compound her poverty.
